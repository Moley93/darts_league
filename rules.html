Crawley Darts League Rules

General Rules and Registration

League Structure: The Crawley Darts League comprises two divisions: Premier Division and A Division, each consisting of up to 8 teams.
Team Registration: Teams must register before the season's start date with a minimum of 8 players and a maximum of 12 players per team.
Registration forms must be submitted to the League Secretary by the deadline specified in the AGM minutes.
Registration fee of £40 per team must be paid before the first match of the season.
Player Registration: Players can only be registered for one team at a time within the Crawley Darts League.
New players can be registered throughout the season by notifying the League Secretary at least 48 hours before they play their first match.
A player may transfer between teams only once per season with the approval of the Committee.
Team Venues: Each team must have a registered venue with at least one board of acceptable standard as determined by the Committee.
The oche must be clearly marked and at a distance of 7 feet 9¼ inches (2.37m) from the face of the board.
The height of the board must be 5 feet 8 inches (1.73m) from the floor to the center of the bullseye.
Match Schedule: Matches are scheduled to be played on Friday evenings at 8:00 PM unless agreed otherwise by both team captains and the Committee.

League Rules

Season Format: Each team will play every other team in their division twice during the season, once at home and once away.
Match Format: Each match consists of 9 games: 3 doubles games and 6 singles games.
Premier Division: All games are best of 5 legs (first to 3).
A Division: All games are best of 3 legs (first to 2).
Points System: Teams receive 1 point for each game won in a match.
A match can end in a draw (e.g., 4-4, 5-4) based on total games won.
League positions are determined by total points, then leg difference, then games won.
Player Participation: A team must field at least 5 players for a match to be valid.
No player may participate in more than one doubles game and one singles game in a match.
Teams failing to field 5 players will forfeit the appropriate number of games.
Postponements: Matches can only be postponed with agreement from both captains and notification to the League Secretary at least 48 hours in advance.
Postponed matches must be played within 2 weeks of the original date.
Any team failing to fulfill a fixture without proper notice will forfeit the match 9-0.
Result Submission: The home team captain is responsible for submitting the match result online within 48 hours of the match being played.
Promotion/Relegation: At the end of each season, the bottom two teams in the Premier Division will be relegated to the A Division, and the top two teams in the A Division will be promoted to the Premier Division.

Cup Competition Rules

Entry: All teams registered in the league are automatically entered into their respective division's cup competition.
Format: The cup competition is a straight knockout tournament.
Draws for each round will be conducted by the Committee and published on the league website.
The team drawn first has home advantage through to the semi-finals.
Match Format: Cup matches follow the same format as league matches: 3 doubles games and 6 singles games.
In the event of a 4-4 tie after all games, a deciding doubles game will be played.
For the deciding game, team captains can select any two players who have played in the match.
Semi-Finals and Final: Semi-finals are played at the home venue of the first team drawn.
The final will be played at a neutral venue selected by the Committee.
All players must have played at least 3 league matches for their team to be eligible for the cup final.
Trophy: The winning team will hold the cup trophy until the following season's final.
Individual trophies will be presented to the members of the winning team.

Match Play Rules

Start Time: Matches should commence by 8:15 PM. A grace period of 30 minutes is allowed for late arrivals.
Order of Play: The three doubles games are played first, followed by the six singles games.
Team captains must provide their full line-up before the start of the doubles games.
No changes to the line-up are permitted once the match has started unless agreed by both captains.
Throw for Bull: To determine who starts each leg, a player from each team throws for the bull.
The player whose dart lands closest to the bullseye will throw first in the first leg.
The visiting team has the option to throw first or second when throwing for the bull.
Subsequent legs follow an alternating format regardless of who won the previous leg.
Warm-up Throws: Each player is allowed up to 6 warm-up darts before their game begins.
Scorers and Markers: Each team shall provide a scorer/marker for alternate games.
Scores must be clearly marked on a scoreboard visible to both players.
A player's score must be announced by the marker before the player removes their darts from the board.
Disputes: In case of a dispute, team captains should try to resolve the issue amicably.
If no agreement can be reached, the match should continue under protest and the matter referred to the Committee for resolution.

Scoring and Player Rankings

Singles Rankings System

Premier Division Scoring: Singles matches are scored as follows:
Win 3-0: 5 points
Win 3-1: 4 points
Win 3-2: 3 points
Loss 2-3: 2 points
Loss 1-3: 1 point
Loss 0-3: 0 points
A Division Scoring: Singles matches are scored as follows:
Win 2-0: 3 points
Win 2-1: 2 points
Loss 1-2: 1 point
Loss 0-2: 0 points
Player Rankings: Players are ranked based on points accumulated in league singles games.
In case of a tie on points, rankings are determined by the number of wins, then by leg difference.
A player must have played a minimum of 5 singles games to be eligible for end-of-season individual awards.

180s and High Finishes

Recording: All 180s and high finishes (100+) must be recorded on the match result form.
180s and high finishes are recorded separately for league and cup matches.
Verification: 180s and high finishes must be witnessed and verified by both team captains.
Awards: End-of-season awards will be presented for:
Most 180s (Premier and A Division)
Highest finish (Premier and A Division)
Player of the season (based on singles rankings in each division)
